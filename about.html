About Shamba Network

Decentralizing climate data to empower the land stewards who feed the world and protect the planet.

Our Mission

Empowering Land Stewards Through Impact Credits

We believe every smallholder farmer deserves access to climate finance, carbon markets, and insurance — regardless of farm size or location.

Climate Insurance

Parametric insurance products that automatically pay out when satellite data detects drought, flood, or extreme weather — no claims filing required.

Carbon Removal

Decentralized MRV enables smallholders to issue verifiable carbon credits from agroforestry, regenerative practices, and land restoration.

Regenerative Finance

On-chain climate data unlocks new financial instruments — green bonds, anticipatory cash transfers, and DeFi products for the world's most vulnerable communities.

Biodiversity Protection

Earth observation data verifies ecosystem health, enabling biodiversity credits and ecological impact monitoring across landscapes.

Inclusive Finance

Breaking barriers for 33 million African smallholder farmers by making quality MRV services accessible and affordable through satellite data and AI.

Land Regeneration

Incentivizing sustainable agriculture through data-driven verification — ensuring farming practices restore rather than deplete the soil.

Our Story

From Research to Revolution

A Personal Mission

Shamba Network was founded in 2021 by Kennedy Ng'ang'a, a geospatial engineer whose own family members are smallholder farmers in Kenya. The research began in 2020, driven by a simple observation: the world generates petabytes of climate data daily, yet almost none of it reaches the people who need it most.

After five years at the International Centre for Tropical Agriculture (CIAT), Kennedy saw firsthand how smallholder farmers — typically farming less than two hectares — were excluded from carbon credit markets and affordable insurance because existing monitoring and verification processes cost $30–50 per hectare.

What started as a centralized oracle delivering satellite data on-chain has evolved into a fully decentralized network where anyone can run a node, compute ecological indicators, and deliver consensus-verified results to smart contracts — with economic incentives that reward accuracy.
